Reviving Restaurant Sales: A Data-Driven Approach Using Predictive Analytics and Business Insights

A predictive analytics project to support a local Thai restaurant's business turnaround

Course: BUS 424 – Customer Intelligence

Introduction

This was a team project aimed to support a family-owned Thai restaurant in Abbotsford that was facing declining sales and customer satisfaction.

Using 300+ days of sales and review data, I contributed to data cleaning, regression modeling, and sentiment analysis. Through EDA, MLR, and NLP, we identified key trends like low weekday sales, strong holiday performance, and a correlation between positive reviews and tips, and developed interactive dashboards for business insights and targeted campaigns.

Tools & Techniques

  • Tools: SQL, RapidMiner, Tableau, Power BI, Python
  • Techniques: Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA), Predictive Modelling, Multiple Linear Regression (MLR), Data visualization, Natural Language Processing (NLP), Sentiment Analysis

Key Features

Built an end-to-end analytics solution to diagnose and resolve performance issues at a local restaurant, integrating structured and unstructured data sources.

  • Collected and cleaned 313 days of sales, weather, and calendar data using SQL and Excel
  • Integrated 400+ Google reviews with sentiment scores using web scraping (Python) and merged with tipping data
  • Created dynamic dashboards for weekly sales, tipping behavior, ratings, and sentiment
  • Built a predictive regression model (RMSE: 276.99) in RapidMiner to estimate daily revenue using variables like day, temperature, and holidays
  • Used NLP to correlate customer sentiment with tipping behavior and visualized trends using word clouds and sentiment charts

    Results & Insights

    • Sales were lowest on Thursdays, highest on Sundays
    • Warm temperatures and holidays increased revenue significantly
    • Negative customer sentiment grew after staff changes and linked to lower tips
    • Word clouds highlighted "delicious" and "friendly" in positives; "messed" and "disappointed" in negatives

    We recommended targeted weekday promotions, sentiment-based dashboards, and basic automation for inventory and staff scheduling. We also proposed QR-based private feedback + discount system to recover customer trust The goal was to improve service quality and customer retention while optimizing operations with minimal overhead.

    Overall, this project strengthened my skills in data storytelling, model validation, and translating business problems into analytical solutions, while also reinforcing the power of analytics in small business decision-making.
